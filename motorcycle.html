Motorcycle

Riding a motorcycle in LA grants a lot of freedom.

A motorcycle always grants more freedom, more life. I love being in a car, driving a car. There is a beautiful intimacy that comes with being in a car, and the relationship you can have with the vehicle. I get it - however, on a motorcycle, that experience can get to 10x enhancement.

Now, assuming ideal weather conditions (non rainy and not too cold); ideal road conditions (lane splitting legal, good roads) and the right skills, a motorcycle ride is a shortcut to getting into flow state in my daily life. Imagine being a Spanish settler in the 1600s riding a horse through unexplored land. Riding a motorcycle is the closest experience we have from that today, and while we may be lacking the 'settlers' excitement, we have added more excitement in terms of miles per hour (wtf is a kilometer). I cannot get over blending into the environemnts I explore, I can feel any slight temperature change on my body, I can smell and feel every single mile on a highway.

It is by far the biggest source of risk in my life. I envision a future in which family responsibilities will keep me from riding so much, if not from riding at all. I hope that day never comes.

I grew up riding scooters with my friends. Honda SH 125.

When I moved to LA, not being able to afford a car, I always opted for a 2-wheel option. I would drive around the westside on a used $700 125cc CaliClassic scooter while attending Santa Monica college, I would commute to USC with a 300cc Kawasaki Versys (del amo deal 5K), and I moved to SF on a black Kawasaki Versys 300-x ($4K from earlier proceeds cash).

When I moved back to LA at 26 years old in a different financial situation, my first purchase was a new Ducati Multistrada V2S, which I currently ride. This has always been my dream bike.
