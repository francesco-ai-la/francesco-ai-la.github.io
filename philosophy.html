Philosophy

One of my closest friends is from Saudi Arabia, and I try to visit him every year. In 2023, during one of my trips there, I met one of his friends with whom I connected very deeply. After speaking with him for a while, I asked what he does in life, and he told me he is a poet.

That answer struck me. I know people still write poems nowadays, but I had always associated the profession of a poet with those who wrote centuries ago—figures I encountered during literature classes. His confidence in asserting his profession through the art he creates was a powerful epiphany for me, as it made me realize the many limitations that Western society imposes today when starting a professional career.

I think that one day, forty years from now, I will be a philosopher. However, I do not want to identify this as my current profession. I want to be someone who earns the right to philosophize through whatever I am able to contribute during my tiny existence in this vast world. Unlike my poet friend—and other friends I have in music, cinema, and other artistic endeavors—I do not believe I have a talent that is worth showing to the world today, apart from the few words on this website that may inspire somebody.

I believe my true form of artistry lies in living an interesting life.
